Modular Makeup Packaging Sculpture

Product Design

In the realm of makeup packaging, functionality and aesthetics often exist in isolation. As a designer and consumer, I noticed the disconnect between these elements, particularly when it comes to travel-friendly solutions. Standard packaging across brands lacks both the utility and the visual appeal that would make makeup storage truly satisfying and economical. This realization sparked a creative challenge: to design a modular, customizable solution that transcends typical packaging constraints. By blending sculptural design with practical features, I aimed to create a product that doesn’t just store makeup.

This project explores how the tactile, aesthetic, and functional aspects of makeup packaging can converge into an accessible and artistic form, turning an everyday necessity into a statement piece that celebrates both beauty and purpose.

Machines, Tools, & Software

  • Epilog Laser Fusion Pro
  • Adobe Illustrator 2024

Materials

  • 1/4" clear acrylic
  • 27mm/37mm/47mm Round Makeup Pans
  • 3.8mm/4.8mm Round Neodymium Magnets

Identifying the Problem


As both a consumer and designer, I noticed makeup packaging falls short in functionality and aesthetics. Traveling with makeup means buying duplicate products, which is costly and inconvenient. The inconsistent design and lack of creativity across brands also make it challenging to organize and uninspiring from a design perspective. I aimed to address these issues by creating a sculptural, modular makeup packaging solution that merges functionality and beauty.

Design Inspiration


I drew inspiration from vintage perfumery, where ornate bottles served both decorative and practical functions. Iconic tabletop lighters from brands like Edie Parker, once reserved for elite settings, influenced my approach to recontextualize everyday items with artistic flair. Other inspirations included the sculptural lip balm packaging by Salwashoo and Cadence’s magnetic travel containers, both of which add aesthetic and functional elements to standard designs.

Solution: A Modular Makeup Sculpture


My final design features 21 clear acrylic oval pieces in varying sizes, held together by magnets to form a customizable, flattened spherical sculpture. The clear acrylic allows visibility of the makeup inside, emphasizing function over brand marking. Seven of these pieces include cutouts designed to fit metal tins of different sizes, holding products like bronzer, blush, and eyeshadow. The arrangement of magnetized pieces enables complete customization, allowing users to stack and organize based on their needs.



Key Features


  • Customizable Layout: The modular design allows users to reconfigure the pieces, adapting for both display and travel. A smaller travel-sized version can be created by stacking one makeup-holding piece between a lid and a base.
  • Portable and Compact: For travel, the central piece, which fits four makeup tins, offers a convenient option to carry multiple products in a single container.
  • Visual Appeal and Functionality: Inspired by vintage aesthetics, the design transforms ordinary makeup packaging into a functional sculpture that enhances vanity decor.

Next Steps for Improvement


If I were to expand this project, I would focus on accessibility, usability, and expanding product options. To make the design more accessible, I would add ridges to the bottom pieces for ease of separation, with space for braille labels to identify products. A numbering system would help users reassemble the original configuration effortlessly, while a one-way mirror film on the top pieces would provide a built-in mirror without obstructing the view of the makeup. For liquid products, I would consider creating a second sculpture or adding magnetized pieces specifically for liquids, allowing for compatibility with the main structure. Ultimately, this design elevates makeup packaging into a functional art piece that combines convenience, accessibility, and an elegant, timeless aesthetic.
