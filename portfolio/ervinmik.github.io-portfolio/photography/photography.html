Depicted is a self-portrait photography project of me taking my hair out of my box braids and then curling my hair with Flexi rods. The process took two days from 1pm on Wednesday to 5pm on Thursday. I decided to take the portraits in front of my wall collage of prominent black artists, photographers, musicians, athletes, celebrities, and art because it's significant to my artistic style and taste. In the photographs, I am wearing a shirt that I designed that depicts a doodle calendar, a collection of pictures I drew every day for a year. The t-shirt design is further featured under the "print" page.

I used a 35mm film camera and an Aputure light storm 60x Bi-Color Focusing light for lighting. I cut and sleeved the negatives after developing and printed the images in the darkroom. I then used an enlarger to project the negative image onto light-sensitive paper that was developed again to produce a final photograph.
