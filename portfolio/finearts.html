As a graphic designer, I rarely get to practice fine art, but still, life drawing has been a passion of mine since high school. This still-life portrait of a bell pepper using oil pastels is one of my favorite works of mine.

After setting up the scene, I sketched the outline lightly with white and blue Craypa pastels. I drew basic shapes such as circles, ovals, and triangles to help you establish the form and proportions.

I then started blocking in colors by filling in the basic shapes and shading to create the three-dimensional form of the bell pepper. I used darker shades of the same colors to create shadows and highlights and then blended them with my fingers for a smooth transition.

To refine details, I added more texture and highlights for light reflection. Overall, drawing a still-life portrait of a bell pepper using oil pastels requires patience and attention to detail. But with practice and experimentation, you can create a stunning piece of artwork that showcases the beauty and complexity of this humble vegetable.
