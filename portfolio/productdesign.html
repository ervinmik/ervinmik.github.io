The interrogative design project I worked on is a hooded hair dryer that blows cool air when a person speaks into a microphone. The purpose of this project is to address the emotional toll of heat damage and societal pressure on black women to have kept or straightened hair. It aims to create a therapeutic vessel for black women to share their hair care journey and find comfort in their hair progress.

The hooded dryer is re-engineered to blow cool air instead of intensely hot air when the speaker talks into a microphone with a hot-comb handle attachment for therapeutic liberty through positive reinforcement. How it works:
1. Dynamic microphone is plugged into the input source
2. Converts sound waves into voltage â€“ The digitized wave voltage form is then sharpened/smoothed
3. The voltage is digitized into information
4. Re-released into amplified voltage
5. The voltage is outputted to power the electric motor (PC fan)

The design concept behind this hooded hair dryer and hot-comb microphone is to allow the speaker to face the sources of their hair struggles and provide a physical representation of the emotional release. The cool air blowing from the dryer serves as a metaphorical and literal way to soothe and comfort as the user speaks freely and openly allowing relief and liberation they may experience in sharing their stories.

The project's message is centered around raising awareness of the struggles faced by black women in maintaining and embracing their natural hair. It aims to create a safe space where they can discuss the challenges they encounter, such as heat damage and societal expectations of black hair, and find support and encouragement from others who may be going through similar experiences. By speaking into the microphone, users can share their personal hair care journey, the challenges they have faced, and the progress they have made in accepting and caring for their natural hair.
